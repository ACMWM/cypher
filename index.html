About Us

Cypher VI is the College of William and Mary's annual hackathon. It's an opportunity for students to come together for a weekend and learn new stuff, create cool projects, and win prizes. We pride ourselves on running an event that is accessible and welcoming to everyone, regardless of their skill or experience levels. We hope that Cypher VI will be a place for people to innovate and develop things that they had never considered before. This year our goal is to focus on sustainability and reducing the environmental impact of our hackathon.

Who's Invited?

Cypher VI is open to anyone and everyone. Whether you've pioneered new machine learning techniques or you don't know the difference between Python and Java, you're welcome at our hackathon. We want people of all interests and backgrounds to attend, so if you've ever even considered learning more about computer science, now is your time to take the dive.

Virtual Hackathon

This year, Cypher VI is completely online! We will be using Discord as the main platform for communication for the event and Youtube livestreams will be used for the opening and closing ceremonies, tech talks, and showing off hacks at the end of the hackathon. You can make a Discord account here. All livestreams will be recorded and available on the Cypher YouTube channel afterwards.

Experience?

Absolutely not! People who haven’t written a single line of code are welcome! We will have workshops at the start of the hackathon to get you started and mentors throughout the hackathon to make sure you get the help you need.

Prizes

To be announced - we've got $3500 in cash and prizes up for grabs!

Who?

Any current college student, or anyone who has graduated in the last 12 months is eligible to attend Cypher.

Teams?

The maximum number of members per team is 4. We’ll have team-building activities at the start of Cypher, so don’t worry if you don’t have a team yet!

Preparations?

Come with ideas, but no code can be written prior to the event.

Rules?

All hackers are subject to the MLH Code of Conduct. Following these rules will help us create an environment that makes everyone feel safe and welcome no matter their background.
